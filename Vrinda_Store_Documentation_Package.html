03 · Presentation Guide

How to Present This Project

A step-by-step guide for presenting these findings confidently — structured as a timeline from pre-meeting preparation through post-meeting follow-up.

Step 1 · 48 Hours Before
Preparation & Dry Run

Review the dashboard one full time end-to-end. Write down 3 "so what" statements — one business decision each chart enables. Know these cold.

Prepare a printed or PDF version of this documentation as your leave-behind. Set up the Excel dashboard on a second screen or large monitor if presenting in person. If virtual, test screen share and resolution on the dashboard file.

Anticipate 3–5 hard questions (see FAQ section below) and prepare your answers.

Step 2 · Opening (2 min)
Set the Stage with a Strong Hook

Open with the one-line summary: "Vrinda Store generated ₹2.12 Crore in 2022 — and this report tells us exactly where it came from, who's buying, and where the next ₹50 Lakhs is hiding."

State what you'll cover: monthly trends, customer demographics, channel performance, and key recommendations. Give the audience the map before the journey.

Step 3 · Charts (12–15 min)
Walk Through Charts in This Order

Recommended presentation sequence for maximum story impact:

1 → Monthly Sales (establish the year arc) → 2 → Gender Split (define the customer) → 5 → Age Group (deepen the customer profile) → 4 → Top Cities (add geography) → 6 → Channels (tell them where to invest) → 7 → Categories (tell them what to sell more of) → 3 → Order Status (close with operations).

For each chart, follow a 3-part pattern: "Here is what the chart shows → here is the key insight → here is what we should do about it." Never present a chart without the "what we should do" part.

Step 4 · Recommendations (5 min)
Present the Top 5 Actionable Recommendations

Synthesise across all charts into prioritised actions. Use the table in the next section. Frame each recommendation with a potential revenue impact estimate where possible.

Invite discussion. Say: "These are my top recommendations based on the data. I'm happy to go deeper on any of these or discuss trade-offs."

Step 5 · Q&A (5–10 min)
Handle Questions with Confidence

Use the FAQ section of this document to prepare. When you don't know an answer, say: "That's a great question. I don't have that data in scope right now, but I can look into it and follow up." Never guess or make up numbers.

If a question is out of scope, acknowledge it and offer to include it in a follow-up analysis.

Step 6 · Within 24 Hours After
Send Follow-Up Email

Send the follow-up email (template provided in the Emails section) within 24 hours of the meeting. Attach this documentation PDF and the Excel dashboard.

Include action owners, deadlines, and next steps in writing. A strong close turns a good presentation into a project that actually gets implemented.

04 · Stakeholder Communication

Tailoring Your Message to Your Audience

Your manager and your client have different goals, different knowledge levels, and different questions. Here's exactly how to communicate with each.

👔
Your Manager
Internal stakeholder · Focused on process, quality, and team performance

What they care about: Was the analysis done correctly? Are the insights defensible? Does it reflect well on the team? What's the next step?

What to emphasise: Methodology, data quality (31K+ rows cleaned and analysed), the rigour behind the pivot tables, and the clear logic from data → insight → recommendation.

Tone: Collaborative and professional. Use phrases like "based on the data," "the analysis shows," and "I'd recommend we present X to the client." Show ownership of the work.

Format: A brief 10-minute walkthrough or written summary (this document). Don't oversell; let the work speak. Flag any limitations or caveats honestly — managers appreciate intellectual honesty.

Key message: "I've completed the annual analysis for Vrinda Store. The key findings are X, Y, Z, and I've identified 5 actionable recommendations we can present to the client."

🏪
The Client (Vrinda Store)
External stakeholder · Focused on revenue, growth, and competitive position

What they care about: How do I grow revenue? Who are my best customers? Where should I focus next year? Am I getting good value from this work?

What to emphasise: Business outcomes, not analysis process. Lead with revenue numbers, customer insights, and concrete recommendations. They don't need to know how you built the pivot tables — they need to know what to do.

Tone: Confident, business-oriented, and optimistic. Translate data into plain business language — never use "pivot table" or "data cleaning" with the client. Say "our analysis of 31,000 orders" instead.

Format: Dashboard walk-through (15–20 min) + recommendations table + leave-behind document. Use the Excel dashboard visually — charts are more persuasive than tables for clients.

Key message: "Your top opportunity for growth in 2023 is expanding your Sets collection, doubling down on Amazon, and targeting women aged 30–49 with campaigns in Bengaluru and Hyderabad."

06 · FAQ

Anticipated Questions & Model Answers

Prepare for these questions before your presentation. These represent the most likely challenges from a well-engaged manager or client.

Why is March the peak month — is it a seasonal trend or a one-off?
March aligns with the end of winter and the start of the summer/wedding season in India, which typically drives ethnic wear purchases. It also corresponds with end-of-financial-year shopping. To confirm it as a seasonal trend, we would need 2020 and 2021 data to compare. Recommend tracking this in 2023 to validate.
Why is there a 4-month sales decline from September to December?
The Sep–Dec dip may seem counterintuitive given the festive season (Navratri, Dussehra, Diwali, Christmas). Possible explanations: competitor discounting capturing demand, inventory constraints on popular Sets/Kurtas, or underinvestment in festive marketing campaigns. This is a priority investigation area and a clear opportunity for 2023.
What is the average order value, and how can we increase it?
Average order value across all channels is approximately ₹684 (₹2.12 Cr ÷ 31,047 orders). Strategies to increase AOV: introduce bundle deals (e.g., Kurta + Dupatta sets), add recommended pairings at checkout, introduce minimum-spend free shipping thresholds, and upsell premium or embellished versions of top-selling SKUs.
Why is the return rate higher than cancellation? What's causing returns?
Returns (3.4%) exceeding cancellations (2.7%) typically indicates a product-expectation mismatch — customers receive the item and decide it doesn't match the listing (wrong size, colour, or quality). To diagnose: analyse return reasons from channel dashboards (Amazon, Flipkart provide return reason data). Focus on size guide accuracy and product photography quality.
Should we drop smaller channels like Meesho or Nalli?
Not immediately. Nalli serves a distinct saree-buying audience unlikely to shop on Amazon or Myntra — it protects a niche. Meesho targets value-segment buyers in Tier 2/3 cities — a different geography from the Top 5 cities. Both have low seller-management overhead. The recommendation is to maintain but not actively invest until they show growth signals. Ajio, despite lower volume, has a premium audience worth nurturing.
How confident are we in this data? Is there any missing or inaccurate data?
The dataset of 31,047 orders covers a full calendar year and all major channels, which gives us high confidence in the aggregate findings. Age groups are derived from a formula (Teenager <30, Adult 30–49, Senior 50+), which is a reasonable but simplified segmentation. Month labels are formula-derived from order dates. The dataset appears complete with no major gaps observed. Any specific data quality questions should be directed to the source system export.
What should our 2023 target revenue be?
Based on 2022 performance of ₹2.12 Crore, a realistic growth target would be ₹2.5–2.7 Crore (18–27% growth) if the 5 recommendations are actioned: expanded Sets collection, higher Amazon ad investment, focused marketing to Adult Women in Top 5 cities, a dedicated Q4 festive campaign, and reduced returns. A conservative target without new investment would be ₹2.2–2.3 Crore (flat-to-modest organic growth).
